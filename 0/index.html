Project 0 — Perspective and Focal Length

Goal: build intuition for how camera distance and focal length change perspective and the apparent geometry of a scene.

Part 1 — Selfie: Wrong vs Right

Why this happens: Perspective distortion comes from camera position, not the zoom itself. When the camera is very close, the nose is much nearer to the lens than the ears, so it grows relative to the rest of the face. Stepping back reduces those distance differences and the zoom just restores framing without re‑introducing the distortion.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Why this happens: From far away, rays to near and far objects are nearly parallel, so their sizes don’t change much with depth and the view feels flatter. Up close with a wide field of view, lines converge more and near objects grow faster than far ones, exaggerating depth.

Part 3 — Dolly Zoom

Why this happens: Keeping the subject’s size fixed (by zoom) while changing the camera distance changes the perspective of the background. As you move back, background magnification increases relative to the subject, producing the classic “Vertigo” effect.
